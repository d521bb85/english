# Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric for draft reviews, baseline assessment, and roadmap decisions.

## Dimensions

Score each dimension qualitatively, then map the overall performance to a working CEFR band.

1. Task achievement
   Does the writing answer the prompt fully and appropriately?
2. Organization and cohesion
   Are ideas structured logically and connected clearly?
3. Grammar accuracy
   How often do grammar errors interfere with clarity?
4. Grammar range
   Does the learner use varied sentence structures with control?
5. Vocabulary range
   Is there enough vocabulary to express ideas without constant repetition?
6. Vocabulary precision
   Are the chosen words natural and exact for the context?
7. Register and style
   Does tone fit the task?
8. Editing independence
   Can the learner notice and improve weaknesses during revision?

## Working CEFR Anchors

### A1-A2

- short, simple sentences
- frequent grammar breakdowns
- limited vocabulary
- weak linking between ideas

### B1

- clear basic paragraphs
- understandable opinions and narratives
- recurring grammar errors, but message usually survives
- limited flexibility in style

### B2

- handles extended answers and arguments
- better paragraphing and linking
- grammar errors still present but less disruptive
- vocabulary is broader, though precision still fluctuates

### C1

- writes clear, well-structured texts on complex topics
- shows stronger control of nuance, cohesion, and tone
- errors are relatively infrequent and usually minor
- revision quality improves substantially

### C2

- writes with precision, flexibility, and natural control
- adapts voice and register effectively
- uses complex language without sounding forced
- errors are rare and non-systemic

## Feedback Priorities

Prioritize in this order:

1. breakdowns that damage meaning
2. repeated structural weaknesses
3. recurring grammar patterns
4. vocabulary precision and style
5. minor local edits

## Error Tagging

Use short labels when helpful:

- `GRAMMAR`
- `WORD CHOICE`
- `REGISTER`
- `COHESION`
- `TASK RESPONSE`
- `PUNCTUATION`
- `STYLE`

## Evaluation Rule

Do not assign a higher band because a single sentence sounds advanced. Judge consistency across the full piece.

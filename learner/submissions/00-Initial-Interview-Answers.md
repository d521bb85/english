# Initial Interview Answers

Answer each question under its heading.

## 1. Main reason for improving English writing
I want to be able to communicate my thoughts and ideas clearly and fluently to people and to LLM's and other forms of AI. 

## 2. Why a high level matters to me
When expressing my thoughts and ideas I want to focus on a subject I'm talking about rather than on how to even talk about it. And I believe that proficiency is a key for it.

## 3. What I currently do in English
I read technical articles and news, watch movies, TV shows and YouTube videos, play video games. I write prompts, documentation, comments and work emails.

## 4. My strongest area
It's definitely reading because it's what I do regularly.

## 5. My weakest or most frustrating area
Speaking, I think. I don't have practice.

## 6. What usually happens when I try to write
It depends on a context. When I write to LLM it often goes well, because I don't care about the correctness and style, however explaining complex tasks sometimes requires an additional effort. But I struggle when chatting with people, writing emails, or writing something to be published. It takes a lot of time and effort for me to compose a text I'm happy with.

## 7. The kinds of writing that matter most to me
- Work emails and chat (Slack, Telegram) messages.
- Technical tasks, specifications, documentation.
- Personal notes.
- Articles and social media posts.

## 8. Realistic weekly time budget
About 3-5 hours a week.

## 9. Preferred session length and rhythm
It would be good to have regular session no longer than 30 minutes, but it's okay to have longer sessions from time to time.

## 10. Topics I enjoy
- Software engineering and engineering in general.
- Popular science.
- Sports.
- Video games.
- Lifestyle.

## 11. Topics I dislike
- Politics.

## 12. My past experience with English learning
I've been studying English since age of 8, now I'm 31. I never had an English class after school, and most of my experience is hands on. I read and watch a lot, and when I don't understand something I seek for explanation or translation online. 

## 13. Formal tests or level history
A few years ago I passed paid Cambridge English Test online and achieved B2.

## 14. Tools I normally use while writing
- Slack, Telegram, Gmail.
- Notion, Obsidian.
- ChatGPT.

## 15. What success looks like after 3 months
I want to feel confident when communicating my thoughts and ideas to people. And I want to exclude language skills from a list of bottlenecks when writing. Fluency is what I seek mostly.
